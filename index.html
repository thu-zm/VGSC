Audio Generation Staying Clean In The Mud Is All Foley To Need


Meng Zhang, Baolong Gao, Jiasheng Lu, Mengyu Yang, Tongtong Zhang

Abstract. Rapid advancement in video generation has spurred a surging demand for automated video dubbing. While current video-to-audio models have achieved notable progress in semantic consistency and audio-visual synchronization, they are critically hampered by poor audio quality. This issue stems from the inherently noisy nature of their training data, resulting in audio filled with background noise and artifacts, making them inadequate for cinema-grade sound production. To address this challenge, we propose AGSC, an innovative training-free approach that effectively reduces noise contamination in the generated audio during the inference process. To the best of our knowledge, our method is the first to leverage the inference stage to enhance the audio quality of video-to-audio generation, all while maintaining state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in semantic consistency and audio-visual synchronization. We introduce the Latent Activity Map (LAM), which models the semantic activity in the latent space to suppress the generation of background noise. Experimental results demonstrate that our method achieves superior performance in terms of audio quality, while maintaining strong semantic consistency and audio-visual synchronization.
